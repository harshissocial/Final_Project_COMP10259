Siamese Fighting Fish

Dive into the captivating world of Siamese Fighting Fish, popularly known by aquarium enthusiasts as the betta fish. Famous for their stunning beauty and fascinating behavior. These vibrant fish are highly territorial, exhibiting complex displays of aggression, yet they can coexist peacefully with various tank mates. Betta fish come in a variety of mesmerizing colors and tail variations, with each design unique and breathtaking. Bettas possess a labyrinth organ, allowing them to breathe air directly from the surface, making them adaptable to low-oxygen environments.

Preys and Feeding

Bettas are carnivorous and consume zooplankton, brine shrimp, daphnia, and other small crustaceans, bloodworms, and the larvae of aquatic insects, including that mosquitos. The two biggest threats to wild bettas are habitat destruction and pollution.

Predators and Threats

The two biggest threats to wild bettas are habitat destruction and pollution. Its predators are larger fish, cats, newts, salamanders, and birds. Humans also reduce their numbers in the wild by capturing them to house them in captivity as pets or for fighting competitions with other males

More about eating habits

In the wild, they predominately feed upon insects and larvae, but also enjoy algae that are on the water's surface (Hargrove, 1999). Some of these organisms that the betta fish prey on include mosquitos, earthworms, shrimp, grasshoppers, blue-green algae and spirulina algae.
